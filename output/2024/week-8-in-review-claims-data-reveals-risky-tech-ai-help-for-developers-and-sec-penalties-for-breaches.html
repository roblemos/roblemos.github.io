"Week 8 in Review: Claims data reveals risky tech, AI help for developers, and SEC penalties for breaches"

February 23, 2024 by Robert Lemos Categories : 2024

- week-in-review

Six articles were published this week, with topics ranging from AI tools for developers easing workloads, to insurers seeing the impact of some cybersecurity technologies on claims data. Finally, the SEC material impact rules have made it hard for companies to gauge the potential penalties for failing to disclose breaches.

Orgs Face Major SEC Penalties for Failing to Disclose Breaches

In what could be an enforcement nightmare, potentially millions of dollars in fines, reputational damage, shareholder lawsuits, and other penalties await companies that fail to comply with the SEC's new data-breach disclosure rules. (23 February 2024)

Alarm Over GenAI Risk Fuels Security Spending in Middle East & Africa

Organizations boost cybersecurity budgets to tackle data-privacy and cloud-security threats amid speedy adoption of generative AI.

Insurers Use Claims Data to Recommend Cybersecurity Technologies

Policy holders using certain technologies — such as managed detection and response (MDR) services, Google Workspace, and email security gateways — gain premium discounts from cyber insurers. (22 February 2024)

Iran-Backed Charming Kitten Stages Fake Webinar Platform to Ensnare Targets

The latest ploy by the APT also known as Charming Cypress targets policy experts in the Middle East, Europe, and the US. (22 February 2024)

AI-Generated Patches Could Ease Developer, Operations Workload

Using information from a common technique for finding vulnerabilities, Google's Gemini LLM can produce patches for 15% of such bugs. And it's not the only way to help automate bug fixing. (20 February 2024)

Misconfigured Custom Salesforce Apps Expose Corporate Data

Enterprises typically use the Java-like programming language to customize their Salesforce instances, but attackers are hunting for vulnerabilities in the apps. (20 February 2024)
